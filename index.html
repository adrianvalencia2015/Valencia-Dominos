📖 How to Play — All Fives
›
Objective
  • Play in teams of 2. Partners sit across from each other.
  • Score points by making the open ends of the layout add up to a multiple of 5.
  • First team to reach 250 points wins.
Players & Setup
  • 4 to 8 players playing in teams of 2. Partners sit across from each other.
  • Use a standard double-six set (28 dominoes). Draw your hand and keep it hidden.
  • The player to the left of the shuffler goes first. Play continues clockwise.
Taking a Turn
  • On your turn, add one tile to any open end of the layout. The number played must match the open end it connects to.
  • If you can't play, draw from the boneyard one tile at a time until you can. If the boneyard is empty, pass your turn.
  • You cannot pass if you have a valid play.
Team strategy: You can't talk about your hand, but you can set up open ends that score for your partner. Pay attention to what ends your teammate needs.
The Spinner & Doubles
  • The first double played is the spinner — the layout can branch off it in multiple directions.
  • Later doubles are not new spinners. The line continues straight through them.
  • The exposed ends of all doubles count toward the open-end total.
Key: Until both ends of the spinner are covered, only the two exposed ends count. Once covered, all 4 sides of the spinner are live.
Scoring During Play
  • After every play, add up all the open ends of the layout.
  • If that total is a multiple of 5, your team scores that exact number of points.
Open ends: 3 + 2 = 5 pts  ·  6 + 4 = 10 pts  ·  5 + 10 + 5 = 20 pts  ·  4 + 3 = 0 pts (not a multiple of 5)
End of Round — Someone Goes Out
  • A round ends when a player plays their last tile.
  • Count all remaining pips in the opponents' hands. Round to the nearest 5.
  • Award those points to the winning team as bonus points.
Opponents hold 27 pips → round to 25 → winning team scores 25 bonus pts
Rounding: 1–2 → 0  ·  3–7 → 5  ·  8–12 → 10  ·  13–17 → 15, and so on.
End of Round — Blocked
  • If no one can play and the boneyard is empty, the game is blocked.
  • The team with the fewest pips in hand wins the round.
  • Count all other teams' remaining pips, round to the nearest 5, and award those points to the winning team.
Winning
  • First team to reach 250 points wins.
  • You can win mid-round — as soon as you hit 250 during play, the game is over.
  • If both teams hit 250 in the same round, the higher score wins.
📖 How to Play — Chicken Foot
›
Objective
  • Get rid of as many dominoes as possible each round.
  • Finish with the lowest score — scored by counting pips left in your hand.
  • After 10 rounds, the player with the lowest total wins.
Equipment
  • 55 Double-9 dominoes — all pairs from 0–0 to 9–9.
  • A centerpiece (optional) for the starting double placement.
  • A scorepad to track points each round.
How Many Tiles to Draw
  • 2 players: 21 tiles each
  • 3 players: 14 tiles each
  • 4 players: 11 tiles each
  • 5 players: 8 tiles each
  • 6 players: 7 tiles each
  • 7 players: 6 tiles each
  • 8 players: 5 tiles each
Remaining tiles go face-down as the chicken yard — the draw pile for when you can't play.
Starting Each Round
  • Round 1 starts with the double-9 placed in the center. Each subsequent round uses the next lower double (8, 7, 6... down to 0) — 10 rounds total.
  • The player holding that round's starting double goes first. If nobody has it, all players draw one tile at a time from the chicken yard simultaneously until it's found. That player starts.
  • Play proceeds clockwise. Each player tries to match the starting double. If they can't, draw one tile from the chicken yard and play if possible. If not, say "Pass."
  • This continues until all sides of the center double are filled before normal play begins.
Normal Play
  • Once the center is filled, players build off any exposed domino end — numbers must match.
  • If you can't play, draw one tile from the chicken yard. If it plays, play it immediately. If not, say "Pass."
  • If the chicken yard is empty and you can't play, say "Pass."
  • You cannot pass if you have a valid play.
The Chickenfoot
  • When a player plays any double tile, place it perpendicular to the tile it connects to.
  • The player calls out "Chickie [number]!" — e.g., "Chickie Fives!" for a double-5.
  • Normal play pauses. The next three plays must all come off that double, forming the chickenfoot shape.
  • If you can't play on the foot, draw from the chicken yard and play if possible. If not, pass.
  • Once all three ends of the foot are covered, normal play resumes from any exposed end.
Key rule: While a chickenfoot is open, every player must play on the foot — no other plays allowed until it's closed out with three tiles.
Ending a Round
  • A round ends when a player plays their last tile, or when no one can play and the chicken yard is empty.
  • Each player counts the total pips on their remaining tiles. That number is their score for the round.
  • The player who went out scores 0.
Optional rule: The double-blank (0–0), if left unplayed, counts as 50 points.
Winning
  • Play all 10 rounds (double-9 down to double-0).
  • Add up each player's round scores.
  • The player with the lowest total score wins!
Goal: Empty your hand fast. Every pip you're still holding when someone goes out counts against you.
